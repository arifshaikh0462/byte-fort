Gmail Hacking (Defense First)

Email is the #1 initial access vector. Understanding how attackers abuse trust, tokens, and weak settings helps you lock down accounts permanently.

Common Attack Paths

  • Phishing & OAuth Consent: Fake login pages or apps that trick you into granting data access.
  • Credential Stuffing: Reusing passwords across sites lets attackers try leaked combos.
  • Session Hijacking: Stolen cookies from infected devices bypass passwords.
  • SIM‑swap for OTP: Phone number takeover defeats SMS‑based 2FA.

Defense Checklist

  • Turn on 2‑Step Verification with passkeys or an authenticator app (avoid SMS where possible).
  • Use a unique, long password and a password manager.
  • Review Security Checkup: devices, third‑party apps, forwarding filters, and recovery options.
  • Harden the browser: auto‑update, disable risky extensions, isolate work/personal profiles.
  • Device hygiene: OS updates, reputable antivirus, full‑disk encryption.

Practice

Identify and rewrite a phishing email into a safe template. Then, enable passkeys and remove old app passwords you no longer need.
